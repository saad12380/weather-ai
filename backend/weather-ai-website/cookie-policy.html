1. What Are Cookies and Similar Technologies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile) when you visit our website. They help us provide you with a better experience by remembering your preferences and understanding how you use our platform.

1.1 Types of Technologies We Use

Cookies
Required

Small text files stored on your device that help remember your preferences and login status.

Local Storage
Optional

Browser storage that allows us to save larger amounts of data locally on your device.

Web Beacons
Optional

Tiny graphics that help us understand user behavior and campaign effectiveness.

Session Storage
Required

Temporary storage that lasts only for your current browser session.

1.2 How Long Do Cookies Last?

Cookies have different lifespans based on their purpose:

  • Session Cookies: Temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser
  • Persistent Cookies: Remain on your device for a set period (days, months, or years) unless deleted
  • First-Party Cookies: Set by Weather AI directly on our domain
  • Third-Party Cookies: Set by trusted partners and services we integrate with

2. Types of Cookies We Use

We use different types of cookies for various purposes. Here's a breakdown of each category:

Essential Cookies
Always Active

These cookies are necessary for the website to function and cannot be switched off. They are usually only set in response to actions made by you such as logging in, filling forms, or setting privacy preferences.

Examples: Authentication cookies, security cookies, load balancing cookies.

Functional Cookies
Controllable

These cookies enable the website to provide enhanced functionality and personalization. They may be set by us or by third-party providers whose services we have added to our pages.

Examples: Language preferences, region settings, font size preferences.

Analytics Cookies
Controllable

These cookies allow us to count visits and traffic sources so we can measure and improve the performance of our site. They help us know which pages are popular and see how visitors move around the site.

Examples: Google Analytics cookies, performance tracking cookies.

Marketing Cookies
Controllable

These cookies may be set through our site by our advertising partners. They may be used by those companies to build a profile of your interests and show you relevant advertisements on other sites.

Examples: Social media cookies, advertising network cookies.
